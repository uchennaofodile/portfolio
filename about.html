Uchenna Ofodile

Junior Software Engineer

"I empower people by creating software to help them reach their goals."

My coding journey has been nothing short of a whirlwind. Coming from humble beiginnings, I began learning to code by studying web development on Lynda as I studied HTML, CSS, JavaScript and programming fundamentals. From there, I went on to enroll in a web development bootcamp offered at my local Career Source where I continued to hone my skills in web development tacking on a couple of projects along the way. After graduating from the program, I landed my dream role at IBM as an application developer apprentice; however, after that things began to take a turn for the worse. I started to develop some unexplained health issues, ended up being hospitalized and was forced to leave the apprenticeship.

Left hopeless by the medical community, I had a choice: give up or fight. I chose to fight and after healing myself holistically through diet and lifestyle changes, I am healthier than I have ever been. But my journey is not about just me. My determination and tenacity brought me to where I am today, but I cannot forget the role my family has played in my life and how my experiences shaped me into who I am today.

Studying software development has built character and helped me push past my limits literally and figuratively. Learning software engineering has improved my critical thinking has taught me to use more abstract reasoning to look at concepts from a broad perspective. The path to software engineering is paved with many roads in the sand, all equally valid with some quicker, some seemingly elusive and unattainable, yet despite the unending paths one can take the constant is my unyielding desire to accomplish what I have set out to do. Let's create.
